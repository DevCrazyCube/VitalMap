Three numbers. Every country. 150 years of history.

Hidden inside a single dataset are birth and death records for 200+ countries across 150 years of estimates and 75 years of scientific projections. VitalMap makes that data visible.

01 Births

Live births per year. High rates signal young, growing populations — rising demand for schools, housing, and jobs.

Blue scale on map
02 Deaths

Total deaths per year. Rising rates in an ageing society signal healthcare pressure — a challenge across much of Europe and East Asia.

Red scale on map
03 Natural Growth

Births minus deaths. Positive means the population grows from within. Negative — already happening in several countries — means it shrinks without immigration.

Diverging scale on map

Population is the foundation beneath every policy decision. A country gaining four million people per year needs a very different plan than one losing two hundred thousand. Climate adaptation, pension systems, city planning — all of them are downstream of the birth-death balance.
