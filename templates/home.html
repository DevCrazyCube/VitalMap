Births

The number of live births in a country per year. High birth rates signal young, growing populations — and rising demand for schools, housing, and jobs.

Deaths

Total deaths per year. Rising death rates in an ageing society signal healthcare pressure and pension burden — a challenge for much of Europe and East Asia.

Sequential red scale on map
±

Natural Growth

Births minus deaths. Positive means the population is growing from within. Negative — as already happening in several European nations — means it is shrinking without immigration.

Why demographic change matters

Population is the foundation beneath every policy decision. A country growing rapidly at 4 million births per year needs a very different plan to one losing 200 000 more people than it gains.

Climate adaptation, pension systems, migration policy, city planning — all of them are downstream of the birth-death balance. Understanding the data is the first step to understanding the world.

VitalMap lets you compare any country in any year from 1950 to 2100, switch between metrics instantly, and trace the trend line through history and into the UN's medium-variant projections.

How to use VitalMap

Switch between births, deaths, and natural growth using the metric dropdown. Each gets its own colour scale.
