Births

Every year, hundreds of millions of children are born around the world. In regions where birth rates remain high, young populations drive demand for schools, housing, and economic growth. In others, birth rates have fallen sharply — leaving fewer new entrants to support aging societies.

Deaths

Life expectancy has risen across most of the world over the past century. As a result, the proportion of older people in many populations is growing. The balance between younger and older age groups shapes how economies, healthcare systems, and cities are planned for the long term.

Growth

Natural growth — births minus deaths — is the number beneath everything else. When it turns negative, a population begins to shrink without immigration. That has already happened in dozens of countries. The pattern is visible, country by country, year by year, in the data.
